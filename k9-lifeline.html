Questions That Matter

Are the handlers on salary while on standby, and are there bonuses for actual deployments?

Yes. Standby readiness is the base layer: handlers receive steady living support, and dogs receive full professional care, training, and housing. This is funded continuously by the Mercy Circle. When a team is activated for a real deployment, additional funds are released for travel, operations, handler deployment bonus, and post-mission recovery. Deployments accelerate the funding, they don’t create it from zero.

What happens to the teams between disasters?

They stay sharp. Daily training, advanced skill development, cross-training with other teams, and public education work. The dogs live as working partners with their handlers. This isn’t “sit around waiting.” Readiness is active.

How do we know the teams actually deployed and did the work?

Every activation produces verifiable reports: photos, videos, after-action summaries, and often partner agency attestations. These will eventually live alongside the soulbound records. Holders can see the direct output of their participation.

What about the dogs’ long-term welfare and retirement?

The dogs come from our rescue programs and stay in the family. After their working life, they receive full retirement care (often with their handler). The Eternal level specifically helps ensure lifetime support, not just while they’re useful.

How much actually reaches the team versus overhead?

The design is deliberately lean. The impact wallet flows directly to program costs. We publish allocation breakdowns. The goal is maximum mercy per token, not building large institutions.

These are the real questions. We want the mechanics to be as clear as the heart behind them.
